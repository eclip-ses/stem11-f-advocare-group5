HUMAN VS MACHINE

The Changing Nature of Employment in the Age of AI

Understanding AI's impact on Filipino workers and the future of work

Background of the Social Issue

ü§ñ

Overview of AI in the Workforce

AI is becoming increasingly prevalent in Filipino workplaces. A recent study reports that 86% of Filipino knowledge workers already use AI tools, indicating rapid adoption nationwide. Although over 90% of Philippine businesses have computers and internet access, only around 15% currently utilize AI, revealing a significant gap between digital access and actual AI adoption.

Companies face challenges such as limited skills, insufficient funding, and lack of awareness about AI tools. Globally, AI growth is accelerating due to affordable computing power and automation demand. The Philippines is following this trend as industries like BPO and ICT digitalize to stay competitive.

How AI Transforms Employment

AI is rapidly changing the nature of work in the Philippines. The World Bank estimates that 35‚Äì37% of Filipino jobs are exposed to AI-related risks. However, an IMF analysis shows that about 61% of highly exposed roles are "highly complementary," meaning AI is more likely to support workers rather than fully replace them.

The BPO sector, which contributes over 7% of the country's GDP, faces the greatest disruption due to reliance on routine tasks. Yet AI also creates opportunities: it can save workers hours of manual work weekly and increase productivity and wages. New roles like AI trainers, data analysts, and automation specialists continue to emerge.

Core Problems Identified

Skills Mismatch: Although 86% of knowledge workers use AI tools, many companies haven't provided guided training, leaving workers unprepared. Others lack digital skills entirely and face barriers like inadequate internet connectivity and limited funds.

Ethical Concerns: Organizations using AI for hiring, workflow supervision, and decision-making raise ethical questions. Dependence on "Bring Your Own AI" practices increases the probability of data and algorithmic discrimination, particularly since most organizations lack policies on responsible AI use.

üí°

Why This Social Issue Matters

Changes in employment due to AI present risks to the income stability of numerous Filipino households. The 18-24 age group, where generative AI utilization stands at 64%, faces particular disruption risks while simultaneously engaging with this technology.

According to the World Bank, an estimated 35-37% of jobs at risk from AI are held by the nation's educated youth. To address economic challenges from an unprepared workforce, AI should be employed to promote sustainable economic productivity while upholding the dignity of work.

Primary Audience: Students

Our primary audience centers on Filipino high school and college students beginning to explore their future career paths. These young people are at a crucial moment: the decisions they make now about skills, education, and work will significantly influence how they adapt to an AI-driven labor market.

üåç

Secondary Audience

Our secondary audience includes educators, parents, entry-level job seekers, and policymakers. Educators help equip the upcoming generation with relevant skills. Parents guide youth's career choices. Entry-level job seekers are most immediately threatened or transformed by AI. Policymakers design education, labor, and social protection systems that respond to technological disruption.

üéØ

Why This Audience?

Rapid AI-driven changes in the Philippine labor market directly impact them. An estimated 35‚Äì37% of Philippine jobs are exposed to AI risk. For students just beginning their careers, this means decisions about education, skills, and career paths significantly influence their adaptability and resilience.

Research shows 42% of Filipino workers want to learn AI-related digital skills but face barriers like poor internet access (41%) and lack of time (40%). By targeting students early, we help close this gap before they enter the workforce, giving them knowledge and motivation to prepare proactively.

Jobs Most at Risk from Automation

Many routine and repetitive jobs in the Philippines‚Äîsuch as data entry, clerical work, and basic customer service‚Äîare at higher risk of automation. Since 35‚Äì37% of Philippine jobs are exposed to AI, workers may see their tasks change rapidly. The BPO sector, in particular, faces greater vulnerability due to its reliance on routine tasks.

New Jobs Created Because of AI

Even with automation, AI is creating new roles for Filipino workers. Jobs such as AI trainers, data analysts, and automation specialists continue to grow as companies increasingly rely on digital tools. These roles demonstrate that AI is not only automating tasks but also creating new opportunities for those who upskill.

Skills Now Considered "Future-Proof"

Future-proof skills are abilities that AI cannot replace: creativity, critical thinking, teamwork, and communication. These enable people to remain adaptable in a tech-driven workplace. With many Filipinos using AI tools today, combining human skills with basic digital knowledge is becoming increasingly important for career sustainability.

üì±

Impact of AI on Young Filipino Workers

AI is making a huge impact on how young Filipinos work. In fact, 64% of Filipino employees aged 18-24 report using generative AI. While this provides opportunities for greater productivity, it also means these young workers are more exposed to potential future job disruptions. Between 35-37% of Philippine jobs are considered at risk, putting many young Filipino workers in vulnerable positions.

‚ù§Ô∏è

Human Strengths That AI Cannot Replace

Even with AI becoming more common in Filipino workplaces, people beat AI with irreplaceable qualities: empathy, teamwork, and creativity. These drive new ideas and make humans work well together‚Äîsomething AI cannot replicate. Even though almost half of Filipinos (46%) already use AI at work, it's still humans who make big decisions and lead to success.

‚öñÔ∏è

The Ethical Challenges of AI in the Workplace

One major ethical challenge is protecting workers whose jobs are at risk due to AI‚Äî35-37% of jobs are affected, creating tension between innovation and job security. There's also the fairness challenge: not all Filipino workers have equal access to upskilling, with almost half citing poor internet access and lack of time to learn AI-related skills. Companies must ensure AI is used responsibly and transparently so workers understand how career-affecting decisions are made.

Call to Action

Our Advocacy Message

"AI should empower people, not replace them."

"The future of work is human + machine, working together."

üéØ

What the Advocacy Aims to Achieve

  • Spread awareness about AI's impact on careers
  • Encourage students to gain future-ready skills
  • Promote ethical and responsible adoption of AI
  • Inspire compassion and fairness in technological progress
‚úÖ

What the Audience Should Do

  • Learn new digital and soft skills - Stay competitive in an AI-driven job market
  • Stay informed - Follow changes in the job market and AI developments
  • Support responsible AI use - Advocate for ethical AI practices in workplaces
  • See AI as a tool, not an enemy - Work together with technology for mutual benefit

Key Message

AI is not a new species, but rather a tool to provide additional assistance. AI can never replace the compassion and commitment of a human being. Humans and AI should coexist as a force for change and innovation, rather than one becoming extinct. When we work together, we can achieve more with AI's knowledge combined with our human values and physical actions.
